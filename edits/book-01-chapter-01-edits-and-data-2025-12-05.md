## ADDITIONAL OBSERVATIONS

### Strengths
- Strong character voice differentiation

- Effective tension between Lee and Longstreet
- Historical authenticity in setting and details

### Questions for Consideration
1. **Point of View:** Is this primarily John Walter's POV, or will it shift? The scene reads as third-person limited through John Walter.
2. **Lee's Death:** The chapter title indicates Lee dies at ~10:30 p.m., but the scene shows him arriving and dismounting. Consider timing - does the death happen immediately after this conversation, or later in the tent?
3. **John Walter's Role:** Will he witness Lee's death, or be elsewhere? His position as Longstreet's aide suggests he might be present for the crucial moment.

### Continuity Notes
- John Walter is established as a Fairfax family member
- The scene sets up the tactical disagreement that will be resolved by Lee's death
- Longstreet's exhaustion and concern are established before he must take command
- Lee's physical state (fist clenching, exhaustion) foreshadows his collapse
