## EVALUATION FRAMEWORK

Each element is rated on a 10-point scale:
- **10/10:** Virtually certain, well-documented historical precedent
- **9/10:** Highly plausible, strong historical support
- **8/10:** Very plausible, reasonable assumptions
- **7/10:** Plausible, requires some favorable conditions
- **6/10:** Possible but requires multiple favorable factors
- **5/10:** Borderline, requires significant suspension of disbelief
- **<5/10:** Implausible or requires major revision

## 1. POINT OF DIVERGENCE (1863)

### 1.1 Lee's Death at Gettysburg

**Event:** Robert E. Lee dies of cardiac event, ~10:30 p.m., July 1, 1863

**Plausibility Assessment:** ✅ **9.5/10 - HIGHLY PLAUSIBLE**

**Supporting Evidence:**
- Lee had documented heart trouble (OTL died 1870 of heart disease)
- Exhaustion, stress, and age (56) were all present
- Cardiac events are unpredictable and can occur at any time
- Historical precedent: Stonewall Jackson died of complications from friendly fire (1863)
- Medical realism: Fatal cardiac events in stressed, exhausted 56-year-olds are common

**Minor Concerns:**
- Timing is convenient but not implausible
- No evidence Lee was in immediate danger, but no evidence he wasn't

**Verdict:** ✅ **EXCELLENT** - Medically sound, historically grounded, narratively powerful.
