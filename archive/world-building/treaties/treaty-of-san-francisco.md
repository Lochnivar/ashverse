# Treaty of San Francisco

## **LOCKED CANON - NO CHANGES ALLOWED**

This is the treaty that births the Republic of California.

It is deliberately short, brutally pragmatic, and written in the voice of people who never want to fight each other again.

**Signed:** 14 February 1873  
**Effective:** 4 July 1873  
**Status:** The nicest divorce in history

The U.S. keeps face and bases.

California keeps everything that matters.

And both sides quietly admit  
they're better off without each other.

## The Articles

| Article | Exact Term (Verbatim) | What California Gets | What the U.S. Gets |
|---------|----------------------|---------------------|-------------------|
| **1** | The United States recognises the Republic of California as a sovereign and independent nation. | Full independence | No war, no blockade |
| **2** | Borders: the 1848 or July 1873 boundaries of the former State of California plus southern Nevada to the 116th meridian. | Keeps all the gold, ports, and future oil | Keeps military bases on 99-year lease (San Diego, Mare Island, Presidio of San Francisco) |
| **3** | U.S. military bases remain under 99-year lease at nominal rent ($1 per year). California guarantees free access and defence. | Keeps U.S. Navy in the Pacific without cost | Permanent West-Coast naval footing |

| **5** | Free trade for 25 years, then most-favoured-nation status in perpetuity. | Immediate market access | Keeps California as economic partner |
| **6** | California dollar introduced 1 January 1874, pegged 1:1 to U.S. dollar for 10 years. | Own currency, seigniorage | Smooth transition, no panic |
| **7** | U.S. citizens in California and Californians in U.S. have dual citizenship rights for 10 years. | No forced exile | No brain-drain panic |
| **8** | California inherits all federal property within its borders except the leased bases. | Gets forts, lighthouses, post offices | Keeps only what it needs for defence |

## What California Gives Up (The Price of Freedom)

- Any claim to territory east of the 116th meridian
- Any future military support for U.S. continental wars
- The right to ever re-join the Union without a new treaty

## What California Keeps (The Real Victory)

- All the gold, all the ports, all the future oil
- The right to make its own foreign policy from day one
- The moral high ground: "We left peacefully. You let us."

## The Result

The Treaty of San Francisco is the nicest divorce in history.

The U.S. keeps face and bases.

California keeps everything that matters.

And both sides quietly admit  
they're better off without each other.
