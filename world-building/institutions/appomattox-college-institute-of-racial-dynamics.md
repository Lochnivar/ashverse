## Core Concept

The IRD serves as a passive "mirror" reflecting continental racial and socioeconomic dynamics through annual data publication. Inspired by the Cuba Mirror Effect (1898 photographs that exposed truth without directive), the Institute measures patterns unadorned — offering reflection, not reform.

## Founding and Mission

**Founding (1928):**  
Proposed by a California dean amid Hayseed populism and cross-border economic tensions. USA and CSA faculty approve as a low-stakes monitoring tool. California funds discreetly.

**Charter Mission Statement (LOCKED):**  
> "The Institute of Racial Dynamics exists solely as a mirror to reflect continental social patterns through empirical measurement. We disclaim any official or unofficial role in policy, reform, or judgment. Inspired by the Cuba Mirror Effect, which revealed truth without prescription, we publish data as it is — unadorned, unendorsed, and unaltered. Societies may gaze upon it or turn away; we neither compel nor condemn."

## Structure

- **Faculty:** Rotating 50/50 USA/CSA split; California overseer enforces neutrality.
- **Output:** Annual **Racial Equality Index (REI)** — slim booklet of tables/charts/metrics only. No introductions, conclusions, or recommendations.
- **Metrics:** Wage parity by race/class, intermarriage rates, residential/workplace mixing, estimated economic friction costs (e.g., productivity loss from disparities).
- **Distribution:** Mailed free to governments, newspapers, and alumni on January 1 each year.

## Key Feature: Disclaimers Against Misuse

The IRD actively protests any official/unofficial actions based on REI data, issuing standalone disclaimers when misuse occurs.

**Selected Historical Disclaimers:**

1. **Disclaimer No. 1 (January 1, 1932):**  
   > The Institute protests any official or unofficial actions taken based upon our Racial Equality Index rankings. Reports indicate United States federal agencies have referenced REI metrics in labor guidelines. We disclaim all such linkages. The REI is observation only.

2. **Disclaimer No. 2 (January 1, 1935):**  
   > The Institute protests commercial exploitation of REI data by Confederate industrial consortia adopting urban scores as supplier benchmarks. The REI is not a scorecard for profit or protection.

3. **Disclaimer No. 3 (January 1, 1938):**  
   > The Institute protests the Republic of California's incorporation of REI averages into trade ministry "social score" assessments for cross-border deals. The REI is neither a rating system nor leverage tool. To wield it as such undermines its neutrality.
