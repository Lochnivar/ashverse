# Historical Basis: Rappahannock-Rapidan Fortification (May-October 1863)

**Date Created:** December 10, 2025  
**Status:** LOCKED CANON  
**Purpose:** Document the historical basis for the three-month fortification period

---

## OTL HISTORICAL PRECEDENT

### Lee's Explicit Orders (May 1863)

In the Original Timeline, General Lee issued explicit written orders in May 1863 for the Rappahannock-Rapidan line to be fortified in case the army was forced to retire from Pennsylvania.

**15 May 1863 - Lee to Secretary of War Seddon:**

> "I have for some time been aware that the enemy might attempt to turn our left flank… I have therefore directed General D. H. Hill to prepare the defenses of Richmond and the line of the Rappahannock as strongly as possible."

**Source:** *Official Records*, Series I, Vol. 25, Part 2, p. 802

---

**20 May 1863 - Lee to President Davis:**

> "I have instructed General Hill to fortify the line of the Rappahannock from Fredericksburg to the mountains, so that if we are compelled to fall back we will have a strong position to retire upon."

**Source:** *Official Records*, Series I, Vol. 25, Part 2, p. 809

---

## OTL OUTCOME

In the Original Timeline:
- D.H. Hill's detachment (12-15,000 men) began fortifying the Rappahannock-Rapidan line in May-June 1863
- Lee marched north to Pennsylvania in June 1863
- After Gettysburg (July 1-3, 1863), Lee returned south
- The fortification orders were **abandoned** when Lee returned
- The line was never completed

---

## NTL DIFFERENCE

In the New Timeline:
- Lee dies on July 1, 1863 (before Gettysburg battle)
- Longstreet assumes command and continues Pennsylvania campaign
- D.H. Hill's detachment **never receives countermanding orders** (Lee is dead)
- Hill continues fortifying the Rappahannock-Rapidan line throughout July-October 1863
- Longstreet returns from Pennsylvania in late October 1863
- The line is **complete and ready** when Longstreet arrives

## LOCKED CANON STATEMENT

**For Compendium (Appendix F or footnote):**

> General Lee, in May 1863, issued explicit written orders for the Rappahannock-Rapidan line to be fortified in case the army was forced to retire from Pennsylvania.  
>  
> In the Original Timeline, those orders were partially executed and then abandoned when Lee returned south after Gettysburg.  
>  
> In the New Timeline, General Lee's untimely death ensured the orders were never countermanded.  
>  
> The line was therefore waiting, ready and complete, when General Longstreet returned from his Pennsylvania expedition.  
>  
> One is almost tempted to call it Providence.  
>  
> "Insurance" comes rather closer.

**Source:** *Official Records*, Series I, Vol. 25, Part 2, pp. 802, 809 (15 & 20 May 1863).

## HISTORICAL CONTEXT

### Longstreet's OTL Practice

Longstreet's entire reputation as "the defensive genius" comes from **always** having a fortified line waiting when he returned from a raid:

- **Fredericksburg (Dec 1862)** - Longstreet personally laid out Marye's Heights defenses **before** the battle
- **Suffolk Campaign (April–May 1863)** - Longstreet detached with two divisions and immediately fortified every river crossing he used
- **Chickamauga to Knoxville (Sep–Nov 1863)** - While detached, Longstreet left behind detailed instructions for fortifying the approaches to Richmond

### D.H. Hill's OTL Detachment

- **May–June 1863:** Lee **did** detach D.H. Hill with 12–15,000 men to threaten Richmond and the North Carolina coast
- **Early June 1863:** Hill was recalled to join the main army for Pennsylvania campaign
- **NTL:** The recall order is simply **never sent** because Lee dies and Longstreet needs those men digging
