## PART 1: MEADE'S 36-HOUR FREEZE (LOCKED CANON)

### The Locked Explanation

**Meade's 36-hour freeze (July 2-3, 1863) - 91% Plausibility - LOCKED**

**The Five Factors (Combination Approach):**
1. **Caution:** Meade is naturally cautious (OTL trait)
2. **Lee-death confusion:** Unexpected command change creates uncertainty
3. **Army exhaustion:** Day 1 was bloody, army needs time to reorganize
4. **Cavalry collapse:** Union cavalry exhausted from Day 1, can't pursue immediately
5. **Washington telegraph delay:** Meade seeks approval, Washington response delayed

**The Mechanism:**
- **Dawn, July 2:** Longstreet's withdrawal begins
- **Morning, July 2:** Meade's scouts report withdrawal, but Meade uncertain (feint? trap? Lee dead?)
- **Meade's Decision:** "I need confirmation + reorganization + approval before pursuing"
- **36 Hours:** Meade waits for confirmation + reorganizes army + waits for Washington orders
- **Dawn, July 3:** Meade begins pursuit, but Longstreet has 36-hour head start

**Result:** Longstreet escapes with 36-hour head start, enabling Pennsylvania raid

**NTL (July 2-3, 1863):**

- But Meade freezes for 36 hours
