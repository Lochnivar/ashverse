# Joshua Lawrence Chamberlain (1828-1914)

## Basic Information
- **Full Name:** Joshua Lawrence Chamberlain
- **Birth:** September 8, 1828, Brewer, Maine
- **Death:** February 24, 1914, Portland, Maine (OTL - NTL may differ)
- **Age in 1863:** 35 years old
- **Rank:** Colonel (promoted to Brigadier General during war, Major General post-war)
- **Nickname:** "The Lion of Little Round Top" (OTL), "The Professor" (by troops)

## Physical Description
- **Height:** 5'10" (average height)
- **Build:** Lean, athletic frame, carries himself with dignity
- **Hair:** Dark brown, well-groomed, full beard (trimmed)
- **Eyes:** Brown, intelligent, often appears thoughtful
- **Distinguishing Features:** Strong jaw, clear eyes, often appears scholarly but determined
- **Clothing Style:** Well-maintained uniform, pays attention to appearance; professional military bearing with academic undertones

## Personality

1. **Intellectual** - Thinks deeply about war, strategy, and meaning
2. **Determined** - Once committed, never gives up
3. **Personal Courage** - Leads from front, shares dangers
4. **Idealistic** - Believes in cause, sees war in moral terms
5. **Adaptive** - Learns quickly, adapts to circumstances

### Strengths

### Weaknesses

## Background

### Early Life
- Born in Maine, raised in Brewer
- Educated: Bowdoin College (graduated 1855), Bangor Theological Seminary
- Professor of rhetoric and modern languages at Bowdoin before war
- Married Fanny Adams in 1855 (happy marriage, 5 children)

### Pre-War Career
- Professor at Bowdoin College
- Studied theology, considered ministry
- Joined Maine militia when war started
- Commissioned as Lieutenant Colonel in 20th Maine Infantry

### Civil War (OTL)
- Early service: Antietam, Fredericksburg, Chancellorsville
- Gettysburg: Defended Little Round Top, famous bayonet charge
- Wounded multiple times: Petersburg (1864), nearly died
- Promoted to Brigadier General, then Major General
- Accepted Confederate surrender at Appomattox (as representative)
- Post-war: Governor of Maine, President of Bowdoin College

### NTL Divergence
- **Gettysburg Different** - Battle only one day, different circumstances
- **Still Serves** - Continues throughout war, but different path
- **No Little Round Top Fame** - Different battle circumstances, less legendary status
- **Witnesses Collapse** - Sees Northern will crumble from different perspective
- **Survives War** - Lives to see peace, must find meaning in defeat

- **Not Just Intellectual** - Has courage, not just thinking

### Key Contradictions
- **Idealistic but Realistic** - Believes in cause but sees its flaws
- **Intellectual but Courageous** - Thinks deeply but acts bravely
- **Personal but Professional** - Cares about men but maintains standards
- **Survives but Struggles** - Lives but carries weight

### Writing Tips
- Show his idealism through actions, not just speeches
- Contrast his intellectual approach with more practical commanders
- Use his writing as narrative device - letters, journals, reports
- Show his care for men through small moments
- Let his vulnerability emerge when ideals meet reality
