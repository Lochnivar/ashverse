### ⚠️ MINOR CONCERNS (Not Deal-Breakers)

#### 1. **Reading Destruction Timeline** ✅ FIXED
- **36-hour systematic destruction:** Destroying canal locks, 14 bridges, 200+ locomotives, 2,000 coal cars
- **REALITY CHECK:** Blowing 14 bridges, dozens of canal locks, 200 locomotives, and 2,000 coal cars in 36 hours is extremely ambitious. Real-life examples (e.g., Early's 1864 burning of Chambersburg) took longer for far less.
- **FIX APPLIED:** Stretched to 48-60 hours total (arrive noon 12 July, finish torching by dusk 14 July, march out dawn 15 July). Added narrative detail: "The last bridges were still burning behind them as the column marched southwest."
- **VERDICT:** Now reasonable and maintains the "we're not staying" tension.

#### 2. **Harrisburg Arsenal Size** ✅ FIXED
- **30,000 rifles captured:** Original number
- **REALITY CHECK:** The Harrisburg "arsenal" in 1863 was actually the state military depot at the Capitol complex plus Camp Curtin stores. Real inventories were closer to 12,000–18,000 small arms total (Spring–Summer 1863 figures). 30k is roughly double.
- **FIX APPLIED:** Dropped to 18,000–20,000 rifles and muskets (still a colossal haul—enough to fully re-arm two Confederate corps).
- **VERDICT:** Now historically accurate while maintaining dramatic impact.

#### 3. **Wagon Train Doubling** ✅ ENHANCED
- **900 to 2,200 wagons:** Doubling the wagon train in 7 days is ambitious but possible through requisitioning.
- **REALITY CHECK:** Pennsylvania had thousands of farm and freight wagons in the Cumberland Valley. The limiting factor is teams and drivers, not vehicles.
- **ENHANCEMENT APPLIED:** Added detail that many wagons are driven by impressed Pennsylvania teamsters under guard—adds a nice dark touch and explains the manpower.
- **VERDICT:** Now more believable and adds narrative depth.

### ⚠️ MINOR CONCERNS

#### 1. **Warren's Fall Campaign Timing** ✅ CONFIRMED
- **October-November 1863:** Forced by political pressure
- **REALITY CHECK:** Lincoln absolutely would force an offensive in October–November 1863 if the alternative was letting the Harrisburg/Reading humiliations stand unanswered into an election year. He did exactly that in real 1864 (Grant's Overland Campaign).
- **VERDICT:** Keep. The political pressure is real and overwhelming. Timing is correct.

#### 2. **Union Losses (20-25,000 in 2 weeks)** ✅ FRAMED
- **20-25,000 Union losses in two weeks:** Original concern
- **REALITY CHECK:** High, but not impossible if Longstreet is fighting a series of defensive river-line battles with interior lines and prepared positions. Compare: Fredericksburg (12k) + Chancellorsville (17k) = 29k over ~5 months. This compresses that into 14 days of continuous fighting.
- **FRAMING APPLIED:** Now called "the Fourteen Days' Meat-Grinder" in Northern papers. One corps smashed at the Rapidan, another bled out at the Rappahannock, a third shattered at the Occoquan. Three separate killing grounds, not one continuous battle.
- **VERDICT:** Still catastrophic, still believable, but now properly framed as multiple battles rather than one.
