## NTL DIFFERENCES THAT AFFECT DEPRESSION

### 1. Three Separate Nations (Not One)

**OTL:** Single unified economy with federal response  
**NTL:** Three separate economies (USA, CSA, California) with different policies

**Impact on Depression:**
- **Different timing:** Each nation could experience crisis at different times
- **Different severity:** Some nations might be hit harder than others
- **Different responses:** No unified federal response - each nation responds independently
- **Trade barriers:** Existing tariffs between nations could amplify or dampen effects
- **Currency differences:** Three separate currencies could create exchange rate crises

**Assessment:** Makes depression **less synchronized**, potentially **less severe overall** (one nation's problems don't automatically spread to others), but **more complex** (three separate crises to manage).

---

### 2. California as Independent Oil Power

**OTL:** US oil production, but not dominant global player  
**NTL:** California controls massive oil reserves, sells to Japan, Europe, etc.

**Impact on Depression:**
- **Oil revenue buffer:** California has massive oil income (1873-1939)
- **Less vulnerable:** Oil demand is relatively inelastic
- **Strategic position:** Can leverage oil for economic stability
- **Different crisis:** California might experience different type of economic shock (e.g., oil price collapse, not general depression)

**Assessment:** California likely **avoids worst of depression** or experiences **different type of crisis**. Oil revenue provides buffer, but over-reliance on oil could create vulnerability if oil prices collapse.

---

### 3. Different Trade Relationships

**OTL:** Unified US trade policy  
**NTL:** Three separate trade policies, existing punitive tariffs (USA-CSA), California-Japan relationship

**Impact on Depression:**
- **Existing tariffs:** USA-CSA punitive tariffs (300%) already in place - less room for "tariff war" escalation
- **California trade:** California-Japan trade relationship might be more stable
- **Trade diversification:** Three nations trading with different partners reduces single-point-of-failure risk
- **But:** Trade barriers between USA-CSA could amplify economic isolation

**Assessment:** **Mixed effect** - existing tariffs mean less room for Smoot-Hawley-style escalation, but trade barriers could still amplify crisis. California's diversified trade (Japan, Asia) might provide stability.

---

### 4. Different Banking Systems

**OTL:** Single Federal Reserve system  
**NTL:** Three separate banking systems, three separate currencies

**Impact on Depression:**
- **No unified monetary policy:** Each nation sets its own interest rates, money supply
- **Currency exchange crises:** Could create additional instability
- **Bank run isolation:** Bank failures in one nation don't automatically spread to others
- **But:** Smaller economies might be more vulnerable to capital flight

**Assessment:** **More complex** - three separate systems means three separate potential failures, but also three separate responses. Could be **better** (isolated failures) or **worse** (currency crises, capital flight).

---

### 5. Agricultural Overproduction

**OTL:** US farm crisis, Dust Bowl  
**NTL:** Different agricultural systems:
- **USA:** Industrial agriculture, but smaller territory
- **CSA:** Cotton glut already happened (1880s), agriculture already restructured
- **California:** Diversified agriculture, less vulnerable to single-crop collapse

**Impact on Depression:**
- **CSA:** Already experienced agricultural crisis (cotton glut 1880s) - might be more resilient
- **USA:** Smaller agricultural base, but still vulnerable
- **California:** Diversified, less vulnerable
- **Dust Bowl:** Might still happen (climate, not economic), but affects different nations differently

**Assessment:** **Less severe** - CSA already restructured after cotton glut, California diversified, USA smaller. But Dust Bowl (if it happens) could still devastate affected regions.

### 6. Stock Market Speculation

**OTL:** US stock market crash triggers global crisis  
**NTL:** Three separate stock markets (potentially)

**Impact on Depression:**
- **Isolated crashes:** Stock market crash in one nation doesn't automatically crash others
- **Different timing:** Each market could crash at different times
- **But:** If markets are interconnected (investors in multiple nations), contagion still possible

**Assessment:** **Less synchronized** - three separate markets means three separate potential crashes, but interconnected investors could still create contagion.

### 7. Income Inequality & Consumer Demand

**OTL:** Extreme inequality, weak consumer demand  
**NTL:** Different economic structures:
- **USA:** Industrial, but smaller economy
- **CSA:** Post-slavery restructuring, different class structure
- **California:** Oil wealth, potentially more equal distribution (or more extreme inequality)

**Impact on Depression:**
- **Different inequality patterns:** Each nation has different economic structure
- **Consumer demand:** Varies by nation
- **But:** Fundamental problem (overproduction, weak demand) could still exist in each nation

**Assessment:** **Varies by nation** - each nation has different economic structure, so inequality and demand issues would manifest differently.
