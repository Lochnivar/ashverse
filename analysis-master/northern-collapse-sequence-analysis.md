# Northern Collapse Sequence: Plausibility Analysis

**Date:** December 5, 2025  
**Status:** ANALYSIS  
**Topic:** Sequence of events leading to Northern collapse (1863-1865)

---

## ANALYSIS ASSUMPTION TESTED

**Primary Assumption:** Sequence of events leads to Northern collapse and Confederate independence

**Sub-Assumptions:**
- Each event in sequence is individually plausible
- Events connect logically (causality chain)
- Cumulative psychological impact is convincing
- Overall sequence maintains 70%+ plausibility gate

---

## PARAMETERS

**Key Variables:**
- Timeframe: July 1, 1863 - March 31, 1865
- Sequence: 14 locked events
- Cumulative impact: Psychological + military + political
- Threshold: Each event must meet 70% plausibility gate

**Historical Context:**
- OTL: Union won at Gettysburg, maintained morale
- NTL: No Gettysburg victory, ANV preserved, Northern morale breaks
- Key difference: ANV casualties ~12,000-15,000 (vs OTL ~30,000)

**Constraints:**
- Must maintain chronological order
- Each event must connect logically to previous/next
- Cumulative impact must be convincing
- Final outcome: Treaty of Cincinnati (March 31, 1865)
