## DETAILED NARRATIVE TENSION ANALYSIS

### ERA 1: THE PEACEMAKERS (1866-1878)

#### 1. Stephens (1866-1871) vs. McClellan (1865-1869) - 1866-1869

**Personality Clash:**
- **Stephens:** "Little Alec" - frail, intellectual, moderate, CSA VP who signed peace
- **McClellan:** "Little Mac" - proud, military, peace platform, but faces "lost on purpose" accusations

**Policy Conflict:**
- **Stephens:** Must defend CSA independence, rebuild economy, maintain sovereignty
- **McClellan:** Must prove peace was right, face "lost on purpose" narrative, rebuild Northern economy

**Narrative Tension:**
- **The Irony:** Both signed the peace, but Stephens is defending independence while McClellan is defending his decision
- **The Accusation:** Northern papers: "McClellan let the South go" vs. Southern papers: "Stephens sold us out"
- **The Reality:** Both are moderates trying to make peace work, but both face internal criticism
- **The Scene:** First diplomatic meeting (1867?) - two men who signed the peace, both exhausted, both defensive

**Pain Points:**
- Stephens is dying (dies 1871), knows time is short
- McClellan faces "lost on purpose" accusations from his own party
- Both must prove peace was right, but both face internal opposition

#### 3. Toombs (1867-1871) vs. Seymour (1869-1877) - 1869-1871

**Personality Clash:**
- **Toombs:** "The Old Lion" - fire-eater, re-opens slave trade, defiant
- **Seymour:** Moderate Democrat, peace advocate, but must respond to Toombs Act

**Policy Conflict:**
- **Toombs:** Toombs Act (1867-1868) → re-opens African slave trade → global boycott
- **Seymour:** Must respond to boycott, maintain peace, but can't ignore slave trade

**Narrative Tension:**
- **THE TOOMBS ACT DISASTER:** Toombs re-opens slave trade, global boycott begins, Seymour must respond
- **The Dilemma:** Seymour can't ignore slave trade, but can't start a war either
- **The Response:** USA imposes punitive tariffs, joins boycott, but doesn't declare war
- **The Irony:** Toombs thinks he's saving the CSA, but he's destroying it

**Pain Points:**
- Toombs is defiant, thinks he's right, but he's destroying the CSA economy
- Seymour must balance "we were right about peace" with "we can't ignore this"
- Global boycott begins, cotton prices collapse, CSA economy suffers

**Key Scene:**
- **1867:** Toombs elected, signs Toombs Act (Dec 1867), re-opens slave trade
- **1868:** Global boycott begins (Aug 1868), cotton prices collapse
- **1869:** Seymour imposes punitive tariffs, joins boycott
- **1870:** Toombs realizes his mistake, but it's too late
- **1871:** Toombs leaves office hated, CSA economy in shambles

### ERA 3: THE HAYSEED-PROGRESSIVE CLASH (1896-1914)

#### 12. Watson (1896-1902) vs. Rathbone (1893-1901) - 1896-1901

**Personality Clash:**
- **Watson:** "Tom the Wool-Hat" - first Hayseed president, debt relief, rail regulation
- **Rathbone:** "The Bull Moose" - energetic, CUP reformer, "New Union for a New Century"

**Policy Conflict:**
- **Watson:** Hayseed populist, high tariffs, debt relief, protectionist
- **Rathbone:** CUP progressive, reform, "New Union for a New Century," pro-trade

**Narrative Tension:**
- **THE HAYSEED-PROGRESSIVE CLASH:** Watson (Hayseed populist) vs. Rathbone (CUP progressive) - protectionism vs. reform
- **The Conflict:** Watson wants high tariffs, debt relief, protectionist policies
- **The Response:** Rathbone wants reform, "New Union for a New Century," pro-trade
- **The Irony:** Watson (CSA) is populist, Rathbone (USA) is progressive - both want reform, but different kinds

**Pain Points:**
- Watson is first Hayseed president, must prove Hayseed policies work
- Rathbone is first CUP president, must prove CUP policies work
- Both want reform, but both are from different parties, different nations

**Key Scene:**
- **1896:** Watson takes office, first Hayseed president
- **1897:** Watson imposes high tariffs, debt relief, protectionist policies
- **1898:** Rathbone responds with "New Union for a New Century," pro-trade
- **1899:** Trade war begins, both sides suffer
- **1900:** Both realize they need each other, but can't admit it
