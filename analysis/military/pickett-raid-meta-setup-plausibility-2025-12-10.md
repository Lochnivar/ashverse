**Topic:** The strategic setup and command structure behind Pickett's Lightning Raid (June 14 - July 14, 1864)

---

## ASSERTIONS TO EVALUATE

### Assertion 1: Longstreet as De Facto Commander-in-Chief (East)
- Longstreet becomes what Grant would be in OTL 1864: Commander-in-Chief for Confederate armies in the East, but without the formal title
- Coordinates multiple operations across Eastern Theater
- Strategic control without official promotion

### Assertion 2: McLaws Retains Corps Command, Assigned Deep Raid
- McLaws retains First Corps command (took it evening July 2, 1863)
- Assigned the task of deep raid into Pennsylvania
- Corps-level operation, not just division

### Assertion 3: Pickett's Flying Corps as the Raider
- Pickett's Flying Corps (14,000 men, mounted infantry) is the actual raiding force
- Part of McLaws' Corps operation
- The mounted strike force that executes the deep raid

### Assertion 4: Hood as Diversionary Force
- Hood's Division (or Hood's command) serves as diversionary force
- Purpose: Pull Union forces out of position
- Creates opportunity for Pickett's raid

### Assertion 5: Third Division as Backstop
- The third division (remaining First Corps division) holds key crossing points
- Purpose: Keep escape routes open in case operation turns sour
- Security/insurance for the raid

## ANALYSIS: ASSERTION 1 - LONGSTREET AS DE FACTO COMMANDER-IN-CHIEF (EAST)

### Plausibility Score: 91% (Virtually Certain)

### Supporting Evidence from Canon

**Longstreet's Position (1863-1864):**
- ✅ **Army Commander:** Assumed command of Army of Northern Virginia after Lee's death (July 2, 1863)
- ✅ **Strategic Success:** 10-week Pennsylvania-Maryland raid (July-October 1863) proves his strategy works
- ✅ **Preserves Army:** Maintains ~55,000-58,000 strength (vs. OTL's ~45,000 after Gettysburg)
- ✅ **Defensive Mastery:** Digs in on Rapidan-Rappahannock line (Spring 1864), refuses battle, sends cavalry raids

**Longstreet's Character Traits:**
- ✅ **Strategic Thinking** - Sees the bigger picture beyond individual battles
- ✅ **Methodical and Deliberate** - Plans carefully, coordinates operations
- ✅ **Pragmatic Realist** - Focuses on what's possible, not what's ideal
- ✅ **Logistical Mind** - Understands supply, movement, and practical realities

**Historical Precedent (OTL Grant 1864):**
- ✅ **Grant's Role:** OTL Grant became general-in-chief March 1864, coordinated all Union armies
- ✅ **Eastern Focus:** Grant personally commanded in Virginia while coordinating Western operations
- ✅ **Strategic Control:** Made decisions affecting multiple theaters simultaneously
- ✅ **NTL Parallel:** Longstreet would naturally evolve into similar role after success

**NTL Context (1864):**
- ✅ **Success Breeds Authority:** Longstreet's success (Gettysburg withdrawal, Pennsylvania raid, Hagerstown victory) gives him authority
- ✅ **Davis Trust:** Davis would trust Longstreet's judgment after proving his methods work

- ✅ **Practical Control:** Longstreet coordinates Eastern operations without needing formal promotion
- ✅ **Strategic Argument:** Longstreet emphasizes to Davis that Pickett's raid is necessary to destabilize the North - uses same logic that successfully secured Davis intervention in horse requisitions (February 1-5, 1864)

- Longstreet saved the Confederacy at Gettysburg

**3. Confederate Command Structure:**
- More flexible than Union (less bureaucratic)

- Longstreet's success gives him the authority

**4. Strategic Necessity:**
- Pickett's raid requires coordination with Hood's diversion

**5. Longstreet's Strategic Argument to Davis:**

- **Consistent Strategy:** Longstreet's Pennsylvania raid (July-October 1863) proved this strategy works - "preserve army, break Northern morale"

- **Strategic Continuity:** Longstreet's argument: "We broke their will in 1863 with the Pennsylvania raid. This raid will finish the job. Destabilize the North, and the war ends."
- **Proven Track Record:** Longstreet's success gives his argument credibility; Davis trusts his judgment
