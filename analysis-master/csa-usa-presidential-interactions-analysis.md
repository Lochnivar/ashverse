**Overall Plausibility Score: 90%** (Excellent)

**Key Findings:**

**Era 1: The Peacemakers (1866-1878)**
- Stephens vs McClellan: Both signed peace, both defensive
- Toombs vs McClellan/Seymour: Toombs Act disaster
- **Plausibility: 92%** - Character consistent, policy conflicts realistic

**Era 2: The Cleanup (1878-1890)**
- Breckinridge vs Hendricks: Cleanup cooperation
- Kemper vs English: Gentleman's agreement
- **Plausibility: 88%** - Moderate cooperation plausible

**Era 3: The Abolition Era (1890-1905)**
- Hill vs Harris/Rathbone: "But, Tennessee?" crisis
- Watson vs Rathbone: Hayseed-Progressive clash
- Vardaman vs Hughes: Final legal abolition (1905)
- **Plausibility: 89%** - Policy conflicts realistic, abolition timing plausible

**Era 4: The Trade Normalization (1908-1920)**
- Smith vs Al Smith: First real cooperation
- Underwood vs Wood: WWI neutrality → forced cooperation
- **Plausibility: 87%** - Trade normalization realistic

**Era 5: The Concord (1920-1939)**
- Williams vs Watson: Peak cooperation
- Long vs Watson/Curtis: Trade war → Depression
- Glass vs FDR: Economic cooperation vs conflict
- **Plausibility: 88%** - Cooperation and conflict cycles realistic
