---

## Core Concept

The Institute for Historical Reconciliation Metrics serves as a neutral tracker of how the War of 1861-65 is remembered, taught, and memorialized across the continent. It measures narrative balance against competing myths: "Lost Cause" (CSA) vs. "savior complex" (USA) — quantifying memory rather than prescribing truth.

---

## Founding and Mission

**Founding (1926):**  
Proposed by Appomattox faculty concerned about divergent war narratives hardening into opposing truths. With cadets from both nations learning side-by-side, faculty realize how differently the war is remembered. California funds as "memory awareness tool."

**Charter Mission Statement:**  
> "The Institute for Historical Reconciliation Metrics exists to measure memory, not mandate it. We track how the war is remembered; we do not decide how it should be. Our data reflects the stories nations tell themselves — the narratives that shape future generations."

---

## Structure

- **Faculty:** Rotating 50/50 USA/CSA split; California overseer enforces neutrality
- **Output:** Annual **Memory Divergence Index** — tables and charts of:
  - Textbook bias analysis (keyword counts, narrative framing)
  - Memorial/monument counts by nation
  - Generational memory shifts (how different age cohorts remember the war)
  - Media narrative analysis (newspaper coverage, anniversary speeches)
  - Official holiday observances and ceremonies
- **Distribution:** Mailed free to educational ministries, historical societies, and alumni on January 1 each year

---

## Key Metrics

**The Memory Divergence Index tracks:**
- **Textbook Analysis:** Keyword frequency (e.g., "rebellion" vs. "war for independence"), narrative framing, casualty emphasis
- **Memorial Density:** Number of monuments/memorials per capita in each shard
- **Generational Shifts:** Survey data on how memory changes across age cohorts
- **Media Narratives:** Anniversary coverage, political speeches, editorial positions
- **Ceremonial Practices:** Official holidays, reenactment frequencies, museum displays

**Example Finding (1930):**  
"CSA textbooks: 73% use 'War for Southern Independence,' average casualty ratio (CSA:USA) 1:1.2. USA textbooks: 89% use 'Rebellion' or 'Civil War,' average casualty ratio 1:1.8. Memorial density: CSA 2.3 per 10k population, USA 1.1 per 10k."

---

## Disclaimers Against Misuse

**Disclaimer Example (1935):**  
> The Institute protests adoption of Memory Divergence Index data in educational curricula revisions by either nation. Our data measures memory divergence; it is not a mandate for narrative correction. To use it as such undermines its neutrality and defeats its purpose as a mirror, not a hammer.

---

## Significance and Ironies

- Prevents ideological dominance through data — when both sides see their biases quantified, neither can claim pure truth
- Demonstrates that "reconciliation" doesn't mean "agreement" — just awareness of divergence
- No prescription for "correct" memory — just measurement of what exists
- Appomattox cadets see both narratives daily, making them uniquely aware of memory's constructed nature

**Ashverse Fit:**  
Reinforces "everyone is wrong" theme — neither side has a monopoly on truth, and memory is a battlefield of its own. The Index proves that nations tell themselves what they need to hear, not necessarily what happened.
