## SIMULATION FRAMEWORK

### Purpose
This document provides a granular, turn-based simulation framework for Pickett's Raid (June 14 - July 14, 1864), modeling **realistic, competent Union opposition** and dynamic interactions between Confederate raiders and Union defenders.

**Critical Principle:** The Union is competent, professional, and fights hard. Any Confederate success is earned through audacity, luck, and the inherent advantages of a mounted raiding force ‚Äî never because the North is portrayed as incompetent.

### Simulation Structure
- **Time Scale:** 2-3 day turns (granular enough for tactical decisions, not day-by-day micro-management)
- **Map Scale:** Regional (Pennsylvania, Maryland, Virginia border region)
- **Unit Scale:** Brigade-level (raiders), Division-level (Union response)
- **Focus:** Movement, intelligence, pursuit, blocking, skirmishing, raid effectiveness

## SIMULATION MECHANICS

### Turn Structure

**Each Turn = 2-3 Days**

**Turn Sequence:**
1. **Intelligence Phase:** Both sides gather information
2. **Command Phase:** Orders issued, decisions made
3. **Movement Phase:** Units move (raiders faster, Union slower)
4. **Combat Phase:** Skirmishes, blocking actions, pursuits
5. **Logistics Phase:** Forage, supply, endurance checks
6. **Assessment Phase:** Objectives, casualties, morale

---

### Movement Rates

**Confederate Raiders (Pickett's Flying Corps):**
- **Mounted March:** 25-35 miles/day (good roads, no opposition)
- **Combat Movement:** 10-15 miles/day (dismounted fighting)
- **Forced March:** 40-50 miles/day (exhaustion risk)
- **Terrain Modifiers:**
  - Mountains: -50% speed
  - Rivers: Delay for crossing (1-6 hours)
  - Bad roads: -25% speed

**Union Forces:**
- **Cavalry Pursuit:** 30-40 miles/day (matching raiders)
- **Infantry Response:** 15-20 miles/day (standard march)
- **Railroad Movement:** 100+ miles/day (if rail available)
- **Garrison Forces:** Stationary (defensive positions)

---

### Intelligence & Fog of War

**Confederate Intelligence:**
- **Local Knowledge:** Limited (operating in enemy territory)
- **Scouts:** Cavalry reconnaissance (20-30 mile radius)
- **Civilians:** Hostile (may report to Union)
- **Telegraph:** Cannot use (Union network)

**Union Intelligence:**
- **Telegraph Network:** Rapid communication
- **Local Knowledge:** Excellent (home territory)
- **Civilians:** Supportive (report Confederate movements)
- **Scouts:** Cavalry reconnaissance (30-40 mile radius)
- **Railroad Reports:** Real-time movement tracking

**Intelligence Delays:**
- **Detection:** 6-12 hours (time to report, verify)
- **Assessment:** 12-24 hours (command decision)
- **Response:** 24-48 hours (forces mobilized, moved)

---

### Combat Resolution

**Combat Types:**

1. **Skirmishes (Small Scale):**
   - Raiders vs. Local Militia/Garrison
   - Outcome: Raiders usually win (superior numbers/training)
   - Delay: 2-6 hours
   - Casualties: Light (50-200 per side)

2. **Blocking Actions (Medium Scale):**
   - Raiders vs. Union Cavalry Division
   - Outcome: Depends on terrain, surprise, numbers
   - Delay: 6-12 hours
   - Casualties: Moderate (200-500 per side)

3. **Pursuit Engagements (Large Scale):**
   - Raiders vs. Union Infantry Corps
   - Outcome: Raiders must evade (cannot win pitched battle)
   - Delay: 12-24 hours
   - Casualties: Heavy if caught (500-1,500 per side)

**Combat Factors:**
- **Terrain:** Defenders advantage
- **Surprise:** Attackers advantage
- **Numbers:** Overwhelming force wins
- **Morale:** Raiders (high), Union (variable)

---

### Logistics & Endurance

**Confederate Raiders:**
- **Forage:** Must find food/horse feed daily
- **Endurance:** 30 days operational (canon)
- **Horse Condition:** Degrades with distance/time
- **Supply:** Minimal (what they carry/capture)

**Union Forces:**
- **Supply:** Excellent (home territory, railroads)
- **Endurance:** Unlimited (can rotate forces)
- **Horse Condition:** Can replace horses
- **Logistics:** Full supply network

**Forage Checks:**
- **Rich Farmland:** Easy (Pennsylvania agricultural)
- **Urban Areas:** Difficult (must raid)
- **Mountains:** Very difficult (sparse)
- **Failure:** Horses weaken, speed decreases
