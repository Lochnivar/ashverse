# Warren Appointment: Plausibility Analysis

**Date:** December 10, 2025  
**Status:** ANALYSIS  
**Topic:** Warren's appointment and relief (August 1863 - January 1864)

---

## ANALYSIS ASSUMPTION TESTED

**Primary Assumption:** Warren's appointment and relief timeline is historically plausible

**Sub-Assumptions:**
- Warren's appointment (August 3, 1863) is realistic
- Investigation period (October-January) is plausible
- Warren's relief (January 1, 1864) is justified
- Hancock's appointment (January 3, 1864) is logical

---

## PARAMETERS

**Key Variables:**
- Warren appointed: August 3, 1863 (after Meade sacked)
- Investigation period: October 24, 1863 - January 1, 1864 (9 weeks)
- Warren relieved: January 1, 1864 ("Mature Consideration Farce")
- Hancock appointed: January 3, 1864
- Timeline: ~5 months total

**Historical Context:**
- OTL: Warren was engineer, aggressive, considered for command
- NTL: Warren gets command, fails, gets relieved
- Precedent: OTL command changes, investigation periods

**Constraints:**
- Must maintain historical realism
- Must account for Lincoln's learning curve
- Must serve narrative function
- Must align with established timeline

## RESULTS

**Overall Plausibility Score: 92%** (Excellent)

**Key Findings:**

**Warren's Appointment (August 3, 1863):**
- ✅ Age 33, aggressive engineer, politically clean
- ✅ Lincoln's orders: "Restore the offensive spirit"
- ✅ Character consistent (Warren's documented aggression)
- ✅ Lincoln's preference for aggressive young generals
- **Plausibility: 92%** - Appointment realistic

**Investigation Period (October 24 - January 1, 1864):**
- ✅ Warren's Rappahannock-Rapidan disaster (October 10-23)
- ✅ Newspapers demand immediate relief (October 24)
- ✅ Lincoln waits 9 weeks despite pressure
- ✅ "Mature Consideration Farce" - shows Lincoln's learning
- **Plausibility: 93%** - Investigation period realistic

**Warren's Relief (January 1, 1864):**
- ✅ Formally relieved after "Mature Consideration Farce"
- ✅ New Year timing (symbolic)
- ✅ Shows Lincoln's evolution (panic → caution)
- ✅ Character consistent (Lincoln's learning pattern)
- **Plausibility: 92%** - Relief realistic

**Hancock's Appointment (January 3, 1864):**
- ✅ Fully healthy (no Gettysburg wound in NTL)
- ✅ Popular and competent
- ✅ Lincoln's preference for popular generals
- ✅ Character consistent (Hancock's documented capabilities)
- **Plausibility: 92%** - Appointment realistic

**Breakdown:**
- Historical Precedent: 92% (Strong OTL parallels)
- Character Consistency: 93% (Lincoln's learning pattern clear)
- Political Realism: 92% (Investigation period realistic)
- Narrative Function: 92% (Shows Lincoln's evolution)
