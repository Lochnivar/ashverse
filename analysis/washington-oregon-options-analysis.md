# Washington/Oregon Options: Plausibility Analysis

**Date:** December 5, 2025  
**Status:** ANALYSIS  
**Topic:** Washington and Oregon status options in NTL (1873+)

## ANALYSIS ASSUMPTION TESTED

**Primary Assumption:** Washington and Oregon status options are historically plausible

**Scenarios Tested:**
- Option 1: Secede with California (Pacific Republic/Cascadia)
- Option 2: Stay in USA (semi-autonomous Pacific frontier)
- Option 3: Independent status (separate from both)

## PARAMETERS

**Key Variables:**
- Current canon: Stay in USA (semi-autonomous Pacific frontier)
- California independence: 1873
- Historical context: Oregon Trail settlers (Union-loyal, anti-slavery)
- Geographic factors: Isolation, economic ties, military needs
- Cultural factors: Different from California, Midwestern origins

**Historical Context:**
- OTL: Both states stayed in Union
- NTL: California secedes 1873
- Precedent: OTL state secession patterns

**Constraints:**
- Must maintain historical realism
- Must account for geographic isolation
- Must serve narrative function
- Must align with established timeline

## RESULTS

**Overall Plausibility Score: 85%** (Very Strong)

### Option 1: Secede with California

**Plausibility Score: 80%** (Strong)

**Strengths:**
- ✅ Geographic logic (all Pacific-facing)
- ✅ Economic synergy (combined resources)
- ✅ Military strength (combined forces)
- ✅ Political cohesion (Pacific identity)

**Weaknesses:**
- ⚠️ Cultural divergence (different from California)
- ⚠️ Union loyalty (Oregon Trail settlers)
- ⚠️ Economic ties (Columbia River, British Columbia)

**Decision:** ⚠️ **PLAUSIBLE** - But conflicts with current canon

### Option 2: Stay in USA (Current Canon)

**Plausibility Score: 88%** (Very Strong)

**Strengths:**
- ✅ Union loyalty (Oregon Trail settlers)
- ✅ Economic ties (Columbia River, British Columbia)
- ✅ Military reality (USA needs Puget Sound bases)
- ✅ Cultural identity (separate from California)
- ✅ Current canon status

**Weaknesses:**
- ⚠️ Geographic isolation (cut off from East)
- ⚠️ Economic pressure (California's wealth)

**Decision:** ✅ **ACCEPTED** - Current canon, most plausible

### Option 3: Independent Status

**Plausibility Score: 75%** (Acceptable)

**Strengths:**
- ✅ Geographic isolation supports independence
- ✅ Economic viability (timber, fishing, shipping)
- ✅ Avoids choosing sides

**Weaknesses:**
- ⚠️ Small population (less viable)
- ⚠️ Union loyalty (Oregon Trail settlers)
- ⚠️ Military vulnerability

**Decision:** ⚠️ **PLAUSIBLE** - But less likely than Option 2

## CAVEATS

**Assumptions Required:**
- Current canon (stay in USA) is correct - **Justification:** Most plausible option
- Semi-autonomous status works - **Justification:** Realistic compromise
- Union loyalty overrides economic pressure - **Justification:** Historical precedent

**Limitations:**
- Some details require careful justification
- Economic pressure might change over time
- Status might evolve

**Edge Cases:**
- What if California offers better terms? → **Handled:** Union loyalty overrides
- What if USA can't maintain control? → **Not tested:** Assumes USA maintains control

## FINAL RESULT

**Selected Option:** Option 2 (Stay in USA - Current Canon)  
**Final Plausibility:** 88%  
**Status:** ✅ **LOCKED CANON**

**Justification:** Strong historical precedents, Union loyalty realistic, economic/military factors support USA control. Current canon status is most plausible.
