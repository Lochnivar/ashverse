## REMAINING MINOR CONSIDERATIONS

### 1. **Line 15 - Sentence Structure**
```
"He hadn't meant to imply anything, that he was perfectly willing to draw that sword in defense of the mourner behind him."
```
**Current:** The "anything, that" construction is a bit awkward.

**Options:**
- "He hadn't meant to imply that he was perfectly willing to draw that sword in defense of the mourner behind him."
- "He hadn't meant to imply anything—that he was perfectly willing to draw that sword in defense of the mourner behind him."
- "He hadn't meant to imply he was perfectly willing to draw that sword in defense of the mourner behind him."

**Recommendation:** The first option is cleanest, but the current version is acceptable if you want to keep the pause.

**Current:** The comma after "But" is technically unnecessary but not wrong.
