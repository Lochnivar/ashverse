## Texas & Appomattox: A Maturing Defiance – LOCKED

### Phase 1: Raw Spite (1874–1900)
- **Tone**: Fresh wound. Texas leaves in 1874 angry—Rio Grande neglect, cotton boycott, Richmond's indifference.
- **Reaction to Early Reconciliation Talk**: Dismissive, sarcastic. Texas newspapers call Appomattox precursors "Richmond's vanity project." Diplomats in Monterey snub CSA overtures.
- **Public Rhetoric**: "We don't sit at tables with those who abandoned us."
- **Feel**: Petty to outsiders—Texas is the ex-roommate who badmouths the old apartment.

### Phase 2: Assertive Independence (1900–1919)
- **Tone**: Oil wealth (Spindletop 1901) arrives. Texas no longer needs to prove anything by tearing others down.
- **Texas Military Institute (founded 1885)** matures into a respected, practical academy focused on Gulf defense and border security.
- **Reaction to Appomattox Opening (1909)**: Formal, cool decline.
  > “The Republic of Texas has its own military traditions and priorities. We wish the new college success in its endeavors.”
- **Internal Shift**: Spite fades into self-confidence. Texas officers no longer mock Appomattox—they simply don't care about it.
- **Feel**: Assertive, not bitter. "We have our own school, our own threats, our own future."

### Phase 3: Dignified Maturity (1919–1939+)
- **Tone**: Quartet Accord era. Texas is now the continent's oil titan—wealthy, secure, and quietly indispensable.
- **Stance on Appomattox**: Calm, statesmanlike indifference.
  - No public criticism of the college.
  - Texas officers at Quartet meetings treat Appomattox alumni with professional courtesy (handshakes, shared intelligence).
  - But zero interest in joining: "That's an institution for processing the old war. Our focus is forward—Gulf security, continental trade, external threats."
- **Public Rhetoric (1930s)**: "Texas respects the paths our neighbors have chosen to maintain their peace. We maintain ours differently."
- **Symbolic Gesture**: Texas occasionally sends **non-cadet observers** to Appomattox lectures on neutral topics (e.g., logistics, international law)—showing maturity without compromise.
- **Feel**: Dignitas achieved. Texas isn't boycotting out of hurt feelings—it's choosing sovereignty with quiet confidence.

## Why This Evolution Works
- **Avoids Pettiness**: Early spite is understandable (fresh betrayal), but Texas **outgrows** it as wealth and security grow. By 1939, refusal feels like principled strength, not small-minded grudge.
- **"Don't Mess with Texas" Spirit**: The motto's essence—proud protection of identity—is preserved. Texas defends its independence the way it defends the Rio Grande: firmly, without apology, and with increasing grace.
- **Quartet Dynamics**: Texas's mature stance forces respect:
  - USA: Values Texas alliance against Mexico.
  - California: Sees Texas as fellow successful secessionist.
  - CSA: Grudgingly accepts Texas will never reconcile—learns to work around it.
- **1939 Payoff**: When Europe burns, Texas mobilizes fully under its own command. No Appomattox bond with CSA officers—coordination is professional, not fraternal. The continent unites against the external threat, but Texas remains unmistakably **separate**.
