## CONTEXT: WHY THIS MATTERS

### The Toombs Act's Significance

The Toombs Act is **the single most important economic event** in Book II:
- Re-opens African slave trade (banned since 1808)
- Triggers global boycott (Britain, France, Netherlands)
- Creates 26-year economic isolation ($4.7B lost exports)
- Sets in motion the four mechanisms that kill slavery
- Named for Toombs (suggests he's the author/sponsor)

### The Narrative Logic

**If Toombs is president when Act passes:**
- Fire-eater elected on "restore our rights" platform
- Immediately passes signature legislation
- "Victory high" logic: Just won war, immediately assert independence
- Clean narrative: Elected → Act → Boycott → Disaster

**RESOLVED:** Toombs elected 1867, serves 1867-1871, Act passed Dec 1867 - ✅ CANONICAL
