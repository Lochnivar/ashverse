## INCONSISTENCY 2: Cuba Mirror Effect vs. Global Boycott Relationship

**Status:** ✅ **RESOLVED** - Cuba Mirror is psychological/shame mechanism, NOT economic boycott

**Resolution:**
- **Cuba Mirror Effect (1896-1905):** Psychological/shame mechanism that causes CSA to reflect on its own brutal slavery past
- **Does NOT trigger another boycott** - the 1868 boycott continues, but Cuba Mirror doesn't extend or create new boycott
- **Mechanism 4 of 4:** Cuba Mirror is the psychological/shame mechanism that leads directly to legal abolition (1905)
- **The four mechanisms:**
  1. Northern Raids (1865-1894) - Economic/physical
  2. Global Boycott (1868-1894) - Economic/diplomatic
  3. Cotton Glut (1880s) - Economic/market
  4. Cuba Mirror Effect (1896-1905) - **Psychological/shame** (NOT economic)

**Key Understanding:**
- Cuba Mirror causes CSA to see Weyler's camps as mirror of own past
- Leads directly to legal abolition (1905) - "We will not be Cuba again"
- Not an economic mechanism - it's a shame/psychological mechanism
- The 1868 boycott continues independently (not extended by Cuba Mirror)

## INCONSISTENCY 3: Pacific Alliance Structure - Oregon/Washington Status

**Status:** ✅ **RESOLVED** - Oregon and Washington are NOT part of the US

**Resolution:**
- **Oregon and Washington are NOT US states** - California aggressively lobbies (bribes) them into either:
  1. **Not pursuing statehood** (remain territories and never become states), OR
  2. **Seceding from the US** (become independent nations/territories)
- **California's investment:** ~$1.8 billion (2025-equivalent dollars, 1873-1907) in direct grants, loans at preferential rates, and infrastructure equity to secure Oregon/Washington's accession to Pacific Alliance
- **Mechanism:** California prevents them from becoming US states through economic pressure, bribes, and strategic investment
- **Strategic rationale:** California's fundamental fear - Oregon/Washington represent potential US beachhead to reverse 1873 secession treaty. By preventing statehood/seceding, California removes US ability to establish foothold on Pacific shores
- **Result:** Oregon and Washington join Pacific Alliance (1894-1907) as independent entities (not US states), though they may retain some nominal US connection initially before full independence

**Key Understanding:**
- California bribes/lobbies Oregon/Washington to prevent US statehood or cause secession
- They are NOT US states - they are either independent territories or independent nations
- Pacific Alliance (1894-1907) brings them into California's sphere as independent entities
- California's $1.8B investment secures their loyalty and prevents US beachhead

## INCONSISTENCY 4: Total Raid Count Discrepancy

**Status:** ✅ **RESOLVED** - Raid counts asserted based on plausibility analysis

**Resolution:**
- **Phase 1 (1865-1867): ~50 major raids** ✅ **LOCKED** - Current canon is plausible (8/10)
- **Phase 2 (1867-1894): 300-400 raids total** ✅ **ASSERTED**
  - 1867-1875: 40-60 raids (5-7/year) - High suppression period
  - 1875-1885: 150-200 raids (15-20/year) - Viral spread, moderate suppression
  - 1885-1894: 110-140 raids (12-15/year) - Routine suppression, established pattern
- **Total (1865-1894): 350-450 raids** ✅ **ASSERTED**

**Rationale:**
- Matches historical precedent (Kansas Jayhawkers, Underground Railroad)
- Accounts for suppression cycles (low early, higher middle, moderate late)
- Economically consistent with $1.6B total impact (~$3.5-4M per raid average)
- Sustainable for both organized and viral phases
- Plausibility Score: 9/10
