## PART 2: HOW THE BOYCOTT ACTUALLY HURTS CSA

### The Question

**HOW does the boycott actually hurt CSA economically, not just morally?**

This is the **critical mechanism** - the boycott must have real economic impact, not just moral pressure.

### The Boycott Trigger (August 1868)

**The Sequence:**

3. **British abolitionist societies:** Coordinated campaign - petitions, sermons, dock-worker strikes
4. **British government action (Aug 1868):** Royal Navy "anti-slavery patrol" - any ship touching CSA port is stopped/searched
5. **France + smaller powers follow:** Within 60 days - full diplomatic rupture and trade boycott

**Exact Trigger Moment:**
- **4 July 1868:** Slave ship Wildfire II docks in Charleston with 720 Africans
- **28 July 1868:** Photographs in London papers
- **12 August 1868:** British cabinet votes boycott
- **1 September 1868:** First Royal Navy frigate appears off Charleston

### The Economic Impact Mechanisms

#### **Mechanism 1: Trade Embargo (Direct Impact)**

**What Happens:**
- **British ports closed to CSA ships:** No CSA cotton can enter British ports
- **French ports closed to CSA ships:** No CSA cotton can enter French ports
- **Royal Navy patrols:** Any ship touching CSA port is stopped/searched
- **Result:** CSA can't export cotton to Europe (primary market)

**Economic Impact:**
- **Pre-boycott CSA exports:** ~$200 million/year (1865-1868)
- **Post-boycott CSA exports:** ~$20 million/year (1868-1894) - 90% reduction
- **Lost revenue:** ~$180 million/year × 26 years = ~$4.7 billion total loss

**The Math:**
- **CSA GDP (1868):** ~$1.2 billion
- **Lost exports:** ~$180 million/year = 15% of GDP
- **Impact:** Massive economic contraction, gold outflow, currency devaluation

#### **Mechanism 2: Financial Isolation (Indirect Impact)**

**What Happens:**
- **British banks refuse CSA loans:** No credit for CSA planters/government
- **French banks refuse CSA loans:** No credit for CSA planters/government
- **Insurance companies refuse CSA coverage:** No insurance for CSA ships/cargo
- **Result:** CSA can't access European capital markets

**Economic Impact:**
- **Pre-boycott CSA credit:** ~$50 million/year in European loans
- **Post-boycott CSA credit:** ~$0/year (no European loans)
- **Lost capital:** ~$50 million/year × 26 years = ~$1.3 billion total loss

**The Math:**
- **CSA investment needs:** ~$100 million/year (railroads, infrastructure, expansion)
- **Available capital:** ~$20 million/year (domestic only, no European credit)
- **Impact:** Investment collapse, infrastructure decay, economic stagnation

#### **Mechanism 3: Currency Devaluation (Monetary Impact)**

**What Happens:**
- **CSA can't export:** No foreign currency earnings (gold/silver)
- **CSA imports continue:** Still need European goods (machinery, luxury goods)
- **Gold outflow:** CSA gold reserves drain to pay for imports
- **Currency devaluation:** Confederate dollar loses value (inflation)

**Economic Impact:**
- **Pre-boycott Confederate dollar:** ~$0.90 USD (1865-1868)
- **Post-boycott Confederate dollar:** ~$0.30 USD (1894) - 67% devaluation
- **Inflation:** ~300% price increase (1868-1894)

**The Math:**
- **CSA gold reserves (1868):** ~$100 million
- **Gold outflow:** ~$20 million/year (imports exceed exports)
- **Reserves depleted:** ~1890 (22 years of deficit)
- **Impact:** Currency collapse, hyperinflation, economic crisis

#### **Mechanism 4: Secondary Boycott (Amplifier)**

**What Happens:**

- **Night Riders (1875-1877):** Every lynching = extended boycott
